Plant Life Cycle

The plant life cycle is a fascinating journey that begins with a seed and culminates in the formation of a mature plant capable of reproducing. It starts with germination, where a seed absorbs water and sprouts into a seedling. As the plant grows, it enters the vegetative stage, where it develops roots, stems, and leaves, enabling it to absorb sunlight and nutrients. Following this, the plant transitions into the reproductive stage, where flowers bloom, facilitating pollination and the production of seeds. Once the seeds mature, they are dispersed through various means such as wind, water, or animals, ensuring the cycle continues. This continuous process sustains plant species and is vital for maintaining ecosystems and supporting life on Earth.
